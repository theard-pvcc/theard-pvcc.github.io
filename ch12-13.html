Chapter 12: Models of Computation:

  1. What is the purpose of a model?
    • It gives a simplified replication of a more advanced system to help improve understanding.
  2. List four phenomena of a model:
    1. Captures the essence of the real thing
    2. Probably differs in scale of the real thing
    3. Omits some of the details
    4. Lacks the full funcionality
  3. List the four things a computing agent should be able to do:
    1. Accept input
    2. Store information in memory and retrieve it from memory
    3. Take actions according to algorithm instructions

  4. List the four characteristics of an algorithm:
    1. Be a well-orderd collection
    2. Consist of unambiguous and effectively computable operations

Chapter 13: Simulation & Modeling:

Definge these terms:

  1. Scientific method
    • Observing the behavior of a system and formulating a hypothesis that tries to understand and explain that behavior.
  2. Computational model
    • A physical system that is modeled as a set of mathematical equations and/or algorithmic procedures.
  3. Discrete event simulation
    • Modeling the behavior of a system only at explicit and finite set of times.
  4. List and describe 7 reasons to construct a simulation model
    1. Existence- The system might not yet exist
    2. Physical realization- The system is not constructed from entities that can be represented by physical objects.
    3. Safety- A potentially dangerous/ too dangerous system to physically model.
    4. Speed of construction- It may take too long to build a physical model
    5. Time scale- Some physical systems change to slowly or too quickly
    6. Ethical behavior- Moral and ethical consequences of a physical model
    7. Ease of modification

Illustris: "The Mother of All Computations"

  1. What is Illustris?
    • A model that tracks the evolution of the universe over the last 13.8 billion years
  2. What fields of science were involved in the Illustris computational model?
    • Physics, Astronomy, hydrodynamics, chemistry, and math
  3. What does Illustris simulate?
    • A cube of simulated space that is 350 light years on each side, and contains tens of thousands of galaxies.
  4. How long did the project take?
    • 5 years
  5. How many years of gallactic evolution are simulated in Illustris?
    • 14 billion
  6. Illustris was created using two supercomputers. What is a supercomputer?
    • A computer with an extremely high level of performance compared to a regular computer.
