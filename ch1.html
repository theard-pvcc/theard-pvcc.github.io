Three misconceptions about Computer Science

1. Computer Science is the study of computers.
2. Computer Science is the study of how to write computer programs.
3. Computer Science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software.

Brief definitions:

Computer Science: The study of algorithms.
Algorith: A step-by-step method for accomplishing some task.
Sequential Operation: An instruction to carry out a single well-defined task.
Conditional Operation: Carry out a task based on the answer to a question.
Iterative Operation: Looping instructions.
Stored Program Computer: Computer Model proposed by Von Neumann who suggested storing computer operations in binary values enclosed within the computer.
Programmer: An occupation where one writes command in a high-level language.
Integrated Circuit: Circuit with transistors resistors and capacitors etched onto a piece of silicon.

Computer Generations:

First (1950-1957): first commercial computers, and first symbolic programming languages
Second (1957-1965): Transistors and core memories, first disks for mass storage, first high-level languages, and first operating systems
Third (1965-1975): Integrated circuits, further reduction in size, and increased reliability. First minicomputers
Fourth (1975-1985): Large-scale and very-large-scale Integrated circuits, first microcomputers, computer networks, GUIs
Fifth (1985-?): Ultra-large-scale Integrated circuits, supercomputers and parallel processors, Laptops, tablets, handheld devices

FORTRAN:

FORTRAN stands for Formula Translation.
FORTRAN was developed in the 1950s by IBM.
It is used for numerical weather prediction, finite element analysis, computational fluid dynamics, and many other things.
It is still used today in fields such as forecasting and atmospheric and oceanic studies.

COBOL:

COBOL stands for Common Business-Oriented Language
Codasyl created COBOL in 1959.
It is used for business, finance, and administrative systems
COBOL is still used in the same fields.
